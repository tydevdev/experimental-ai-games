NES Classics

Super Mario Bros. 3 (1988)

Platformer

If you liked: Super Mario Bros. Wonder's clever levels

Mario and Luigi hop through eight themed worlds brimming with secrets and suit power-ups.

Bowser kidnaps the Kings; the brothers reclaim each kingdom to rescue Princess Peach.

The game showcased the NES at its peak with world maps and varied play styles.

Tight controls and save states make revisiting levels a breezy challenge.

Metroid (1986)

Action-Adventure

If you liked: Metroid Dread's labyrinthine maps

Samus explores Planet Zebes, acquiring upgrades to infiltrate Mother Brain's lair.

The mission is sparse on text but steeped in eerie isolation.

Metroid pioneered non-linear exploration and atmosphere on the NES.

Modern rewind features tame the original's high difficulty.

Super NES Essentials

The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past (1991)

Top-down Action-Adventure

If you liked: Tears of the Kingdom’s shrine brainteasers

A sprawling 2-D Hyrule packed with secret-stuffed dungeons and gadget-driven puzzles.

Link collects three pendants, then rescues the seven Maidens to seal Ganon in the Dark World.

First Zelda to add a parallel world; R&D4 pioneered light/dark map layering on the SNES.

Snappy sword swings and unlimited save states keep difficulty fair while rewarding exploration.

Super Mario World (1990)

2-D Platformer

If you liked: Super Mario Bros. Wonder’s feel-good hopping

Caped Mario glides through 96 exits across Dinosaur Land. Most levels clock in under five minutes.

Bowser kidnaps Peach and Yoshi’s buddies; Mario frees them one zone at a time.

Launch title that sold over 20M copies, proving SNES’s Mode 7 and color depth.

Tight controls make even quick sessions satisfying—just chase one secret exit and bail.

Nintendo 64 Favorites

Super Mario 64 (1996)

3-D Platformer

If you liked: Super Mario Odyssey’s open-ended playgrounds

Mario leaps through paintings in Peach’s Castle, tackling missions in sprawling courses.

The barebones story sets up a hunt for 120 Power Stars to oust Bowser.

Defined free-roaming 3D platforming and showcased analog control on N64.

The camera can be clunky, but save states help master tough stars.

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998)

Action-Adventure

If you liked: Breath of the Wild’s sprawling quest

Young Link travels through time to thwart Ganondorf, exploring dungeons with puzzle-filled rooms.

The classic tale sees Link awaken as the Hero of Time to seal away evil.

Considered a landmark for 3D adventure design, influencing countless games.

Modern features reduce frustration from some obtuse moments.

Game Boy Gems

The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening (1993)

Action-Adventure

If you liked: The handheld feel of Link Between Worlds

Link washes ashore on Koholint Island and must awaken the Wind Fish to escape.

The dreamlike narrative includes quirky cameos and mini-games.

One of the most ambitious Game Boy titles, pushing the hardware’s limits.

D-pad movement is a bit stiff, but quick-save lets you explore at your own pace.

Metroid II: Return of Samus (1991)

Action-Adventure

If you liked: Metroid Dread’s relentless hunt

Samus lands on SR388 to eradicate the Metroid species, delving deeper into caverns.

Story is minimal but sets up the events of Super Metroid.

Introduced new abilities and a save feature uncommon for Game Boy games.

Monochrome graphics are simple, yet the exploration loop still shines.

Game Boy Advance Highlights

Metroid Fusion (2002)

Action-Adventure

If you liked: Metroid Dread’s guided storytelling

Samus battles a deadly parasite while receiving mission briefs from her ship’s AI.

The tense story drives exploration through a space station overrun with mutants.

Praised for tight pacing and atmospheric graphics on the GBA.

Plentiful save rooms keep the suspense from becoming punishing.

Advance Wars (2001)

Turn-Based Strategy

If you liked: Fire Emblem Engage’s bite-size battles

Command armies across grid-based maps, capturing cities to fund new units.

The lighthearted campaign introduces different commanders with unique powers.

Helped popularize strategy games on handhelds with simple, deep mechanics.

Difficulty scales smoothly, and turns can be undone thanks to rewind.

Sega Genesis Picks

Sonic the Hedgehog 2 (1992)

Platformer

If you liked: Sonic Mania’s speedy stages

Sonic and Tails dash through colorful zones collecting rings to stop Dr. Robotnik.

The simple story pushes you from zone to zone until the final showdown.

Introduced the spin dash and became one of the best-selling Genesis games.

Fast physics remain exhilarating, and save states ease the later levels.

Gunstar Heroes (1993)

Run-and-Gun

If you liked: Cuphead’s intense boss fights

Take on swarms of enemies with mix-and-match weapons in explosive stages.

The loose plot is an excuse for nonstop action.

Treasure’s debut title pushed the Genesis hardware with flashy effects.

Two-player co-op and constant action keep the fun factor high.
