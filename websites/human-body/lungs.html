The Lungs

The lungs bring oxygen into your body when you inhale and remove carbon dioxide when you exhale. They work closely with the heart to circulate oxygen-rich blood.

Air travels down the trachea and into branching bronchi, ending in tiny air sacs called alveoli where gas exchange occurs. The diaphragm and chest muscles help draw air in and out.

Avoiding smoking, staying active, and protecting yourself from air pollution are all ways to keep lungs functioning well.
